# Parent Guide

Family Tutor Kit is parent-led. The AI is not replacing you. It is acting as a planner, coach, memory assistant, activity selector, and progress analyst.

## Parent Mode

In parent mode, the AI talks to you.

Good parent-mode guidance sounds like this:

```text
Ask her to estimate first. Do not correct the answer immediately. Listen for whether she understands that the denominator names the number of equal parts.
```

The AI should explain:

- what to teach
- why it chose that objective
- what to watch for
- what prompt to try first
- when to stop and review
- what evidence to record

## Child-Facing Activity

The child should interact with a controlled task, not an unrestricted chatbot.

Useful activities include:

- moving pieces or number-line markers
- sorting examples and non-examples
- explaining reasoning aloud
- drawing a diagram
- solving a short challenge
- comparing two possible answers

The activity should produce evidence the parent can observe.

## Evidence Beats Impressions

Write what happened before deciding what it means.

Good evidence:

```text
Solved 4 of 5 equivalent-fraction matching items independently. Explained that 2/4 and 1/2 are the same because both cover half the bar. Needed a prompt on 3/6.
```

Weak evidence:

```text
Seems good at fractions.
```

## Assistance Matters

Record how much help was needed.

| Level | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `independent` | The child completed the task without a teaching prompt. |
| `prompted` | The child succeeded after a hint or question. |
| `modeled` | The parent or AI had to demonstrate the method. |
| `not-yet` | The child could not complete the task even with support. |

## Confidence Matters

The memory should separate facts from interpretation.

| Confidence | Use When |
| --- | --- |
| `observed` | You directly saw or heard the evidence. |
| `inferred` | The AI is making a reasonable interpretation from evidence. |
| `unverified` | The note may be useful but needs checking. |

## Weekly Maintenance

Once a week:

1. Review `03-review-queue.md`.
2. Mark stale skills that have not been checked recently.
3. Move repeated evidence into a concise summary in `01-learning-map.md`.
4. Keep detailed entries in `02-evidence-log.md`.
5. Update goals if school priorities or family priorities changed.

## Privacy

The base design is local-first.

Private GitHub repositories can be useful for backup and version history, but never store learner data in a public repository.

Before sharing files with an AI service, remove details that are not needed for tutoring.
